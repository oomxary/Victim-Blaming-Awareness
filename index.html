Victim Blaming Awareness

What is Victim Blaming?

Victim blaming, though self explanatory, is the act of blaming a victim for their own abuse, instead of acknowleding who the blame for abuse should be put on, which is abusers.

Why are victims blamed?

In some cases, the accused is a well known person, and some people don't want their precious reputation to be ruined, that they'd rather blame someone other than them. In other cases,as according to psychology today, "When bad things happen to good people, it implies that no one is safe, that no matter how good we are, we too could be vulnerable. The idea that misfortune can be random, striking anyone at any time, is a terrifying thought, and yet we are faced every day with evidence that it may be true." In short, to some people, it's just scary that things like sexual assualt, or domestic abuse really do happen, but some people find it as a coping method almost.

What is the mentality of Victim Blamers? How could someone blame someone whos is clearly a victim for their own abuse?

Some victim blamers are abusers themselves, and so by blaming the victim they shift responsiblity off of their actions and onto their victim. Other victim blamers believe that it can make them feel safer, *'If I find something wrong with xyz, like abc, and abc doesn't apply to me, then hjf can't happen to me!' And others, just are misogynists, or those people who think that *'men should always want innapropriate things to happen to them', and that they should *'take it like a man.'

Why is this wrong?

In some cases in society, people will find many things to excuse the actions of men, double the amount of things to excuse the actions of things done to women. This obviously isn't what everyone in America does, but abuse is a concious choice. Men choose to rape women, or in other cases, women choose to rape men, or just to generalize it, abusers choose to abuse their vicitims. Anyone can be a victim, and anyone can be an abuser. Victim blaming contributes to the already toxic way victims are treated, whether it be in court (being drilled into them that their clothing is the cause for what was done to them.) or at home (being told not to press charges because no one will believe them because *'men can't be raped').

What is the solution?

Well, the sad harsh truth of the matter? There isn't one, You can't change everyone's mind. and unfortunately, when most people's minds are made up, that abusers can do no wrong, you can't change it. The only true thing you can do, is cheer louder than those that are booing. Support victims, and work and do your best to get justice against their abusers. Victim blaming is wrong, and now that you've read this, in the words of the immortal Maya Angelou, "“Do the best you can until you know better. Then when you know better, do better.” —Maya Angelou."
